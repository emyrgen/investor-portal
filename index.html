Market Opportunity

Three urgent markets.
One heavy-lift autonomy platform.

EMYRGEN targets missions where helicopters already prove the need: dangerous fire zones, large-scale agriculture, and high-value industrial logistics. The opportunity is to make those missions unmanned, repeatable, and partner-deployable.

Investor thesis

Helicopters are used when the job is too heavy, too remote, or too time-critical for ground crews. EMYRGEN is building the autonomy layer for that world — converting proven aircraft into unmanned mission platforms and monetizing through integration, software, licensing, training, support, and partner deployments.

Existing aviation demandThese mission categories already rely on aircraft-class capability.
Better operating modelUnmanned operations can reduce crew exposure and expand availability.
Platform leverageOne autonomy stack can support multiple mission packages.
Primary Mission

Wildfire Response

Wildfire response is becoming a persistent operating market, not a seasonal edge case.

Why now

Fire conditions are more urgent, aircraft demand is concentrated, and crewed aviation faces risk, cost, and availability constraints.

What EMYRGEN enables

Unmanned heavy-lift aircraft for water delivery, sensing, base-camp logistics, and dangerous-area operations.

Revenue path

Conversion programs, mission software, operator licensing, support contracts, and partner deployment revenue.

Commercial Mission

Agriculture Automation

Large-scale agriculture needs aircraft that can carry meaningful payloads and repeat precision missions across wide acreage.

Why now

Labor constraints, climate pressure, and higher acreage productivity are pushing agricultural aviation toward automation.

What EMYRGEN enables

Spraying, seeding, inspection support, orchard and vineyard operations, livestock-area logistics, and remote farm resupply.

Revenue path

Mission modules, seasonal service deployments, partner operator licensing, software access, and fleet support.

Dual-Use Mission

Logistics + Industrial Response

When roads are blocked, infrastructure is remote, or payloads are urgent, heavy-lift unmanned aircraft become strategic infrastructure.

Why now

Industrial operators, utilities, ports, disaster-response teams, and remote infrastructure projects need faster ways to move critical payloads.

What EMYRGEN enables

Emergency resupply, utility restoration, offshore delivery, port logistics, mining support, disaster response, and remote payload movement.

Revenue path

Deployment partnerships, conversion integration, logistics mission software, fleet licensing, training, support, and partner revenue-share.

Repeatable Engineering Process

How We Convert

A repeatable, eight-stage system. Proven helicopter into autonomous mission aircraft — payload and range preserved, autonomy added.

01
Equipment Removal
Cockpit instruments and pilot-specific hardware are removed from the manned airframe.
Cockpit DismantlementAirframe Prep
02
Sensor Installation
IMU, satellite navigation, servo drives, ERS system, FPV cameras, AI computer, and data-link modem are installed.
IMUData LinkAI Compute
03
System Integration
New onboard equipment is integrated with the helicopter's original mechanical and electrical systems.
ControlsElectricalMechanical
04
Autopilot Customisation
Low- and high-level autopilot is configured for autonomous navigation, GPS-denied flight, and autorotation functions.
AutopilotGPS-DeniedAutorotation
05
Payload Integration
Mission-specific payloads are added, including wildfire tanks, sprayers, cargo hooks, surveillance sensors, or custom systems.
WildfireAgricultureCargo
06
GCS Software
Ground Control Station software is configured as the operator command interface for monitoring and directing missions.
GCSMission ControlTelemetry
07
In-Flight Testing
The fully assembled autonomous unit is flight-tested and adjusted to validate control, navigation, payload behavior, and safety.
Flight TestSystem TuningValidation
08
Operator Training
Training is delivered to UAS operators and ground technicians so the customer can operate and maintain the system.
OperatorsTechniciansHandover
